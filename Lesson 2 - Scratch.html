Scratch

We’re going to use our first control block! Pick the “forever” block in the orange section.

When this block is used, the sprite will continue its movements forever! Just like the block indicates.

The only way to stop the sprite is to use the big red stop button in the corner. This is where we also meet the green flag.

If you look at the control blocks, you can also see another block will a green flag, saying when this flag is clicked. This means that when you click the green flag, or start the program, your sprite will execute whatever commands given.

There are a few other commands that relate to specific conditions or times a sprite will do a command. Try out some of these and see what they do.
